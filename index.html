Research area

My research interests focuses on the transfer in complex fluids flow. The goal is to understand the physics at the core of such liquids, focusing on the competition between thermal, inertial and rheological instabilities. Understanding of these problems have a great importance for the design of efficient industrial process in which rheo-inertial instabilities and thermal flow control can have an affect on mixing enhancement, drag reduction and energy efficiency amongst others. Amir's research contribution is based on advanced lab-scale experiment developments and stability theoretical analysis in order to resolve industrial problems. This in turn helps explain the behaviour of complex systems from a fundamental physical perspective through labratory expriments. His area of expertise is the transfer in complex suspension, focusing on the competition between thermal, inertial and rheological instabilities[11]. He targets the solution to industrial problems and the understanding of complex systems from a fundamental physical perspective

  • Innovative thermal fluids
    • Dispersion and resuspension of particles by buoyancy-driven thermal convection.

Suspension flow

with T. Lacassagne &
  • Particles suspended in the viscous Newtonian base fluid:
    • Particle concentration impact on intensifying primary instability onset and aiding drag reduction.
    • Role of particle size and concentration on mixing and drag forces in process efficiency.
  • Particle-laden viscoelastic fluid:
  • Dilute case:
    • A Delay to onset of Elasto-inertial turbulence (EIT)
    • destructuration of suspension network by reduction of particle/polymer interaction effect
  • Semi-dilute case:
    • A new regime termed here Elasto-Inertial Dissipative replaces EIT. (apparent relaminarization)
    • Particle-to-polymer concentration ratio is a key parameter of the EID behaviour
    • A decrease of Pseudo-Nu-variation against Re-variation => Decay of radial energy transfer
    • Particle-particle and Particle-polymer interactions => polymer stress hardening ?

Complex slurries flow

with C. Nouar & F. Romanò & J. Peixinho & J. C. Baudez
  • Rheo-inertial transition to turbulence :
    • A delay to the onset of turbulent puffs with increasing of yield stress effect
    • A flow asymmetry appear in the pre-transition regime
    • Evolution of the nonlinear asymmetric pipe flow of yield stress fluid
    • Enhancement of drag reduction induced by shear-thinng yeild stress effect

Mixing and heat transfer enhancement at low Reynolds numbers

with L. Royon & P. Brunet & A. Bontemps & H. Hamzehpour & O. Bulliard-Sauret
  • Streaming flow of viscoelastic fluids:
    • Mixing fluids or resuspend particles within a large space, via the streaming flow.
    • Newtonian case: growth and stretching of vortices with increasing of forcing amplitude.
    • Viscoelastic case : a complex structure appears with the formation of a new vortex pattern.
  • Chaotic advection flow :
    • Enhancement of transport phenomena in thermo-fluidic system at low Re-Number.
    • Applying laminar chaotic advection to promote mixing of complex fluids.
